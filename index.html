Biography

I am a first-year graduate student and researcher at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), advised by Prof. Junming Shao. My research primarily focuses on Large Language Model (LLM) alignment, safety, and agent architectures.

Currently, my research interests center around Alignment Algorithms (including GRPO, PPO, and DPO) and Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR). I am deeply interested in investigating the boundaries of LLMs, particularly focusing on the phenomenon of Capability Boundary Collapse during late-stage RL training. Additionally, I explore the intersection of cognitive science and AI, studying agent memory systems for long-horizon execution and representation resolution trade-offs.

On the engineering side, I work extensively with PyTorch, verl (Vulcan Library), vLLM, and Triton for custom kernel optimization.
